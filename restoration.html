The art of Restoration

To become a true artist you must be fluent in every tool of the fine arts — the science of colour, perspective, optics and chemistry. Restoration is where they all meet.

Home/Restoration Department

Mastering academic art is bound to the study of the technique and technology of painting — and to the method of copying the old masters.

The old masters not only made their own paints, varnishes and materials but used them masterfully. Our students follow a complete method that teaches not only drawing, painting, composition and copying, but also the theory and practice of the full complex of restoration and conservation works. Fluency in all of these tools extends a student's professional skill and helps to determine the direction of their art in the future.

The Academy collaborates with restoration artists, museums and restoration centres so that students may learn the basics of restoring works of art and copying the old masters. Under the supervision of experienced restorers, students study the foundations of the profession and witness the restoration process first-hand.

Objective of the course

The student's field of activity includes:

  • Detection and attribution of objects of material culture — determining their artistic and historical value and their category of conservation
  • Organisation and implementation of research and archive findings, and selection of the optimal model of restoration (conservation and reconstruction)
  • Organisation and implementation of the complex of restoration works
  • Preparation of the scientific accounting documentation

Easel paintings are the principal object of study.

Target of the course

Students in the year-long course in Restoration of Easel Paintings are involved in conservation and restoration, scientific research and scientific-methodic activity, projects, and expert work. The precise specification of professional activity is chosen together with the Academy's responsible staff.

Main contents of the course

Technological analysis of easel paintings

  • Basis: canvas, wood, more rarely metal, cardboard or paper
  • Stretcher: defining the type of wood, level of conservation, signs of secondary use
  • Primer: number and colour of layers, analysis to ascertain when the work was made
  • Colour layer: tones and craquelure
  • Varnish coating as an independent layer; texture analysis of the painting

Research laboratories

  • Chemical technologies of restoration — synthetic adhesives and copolymer dispersions
  • Optical physics research
  • Biological research — complex damage diagnosis of artworks
  • Chemical-physical research and the study of painting technology
  • Exploring the parameters of microclimate

The main technological processes

  • Preventive adhesive restoration — solid and fragmented sealing with tissue-paper
  • Strengthening the colour layer by the open method
  • Breakthrough reparation and the creation of new edges
  • Elimination of surface contamination from the varnish film
  • Thinning and harmonisation of overprint varnishes, guided by UV luminescence
  • Working with restoration soil, varnishing, and the tinting of losses
  • Artwork reconstruction and craquelure imitation

Practical assignments

  • Step-by-step restoration of easel paintings of varying difficulty, under a professor's control
  • Autonomous development of a project-plan of conservation and restoration
  • Detection of the technical analysis required to define a painting's condition
  • Compilation of the painting's restoration passport
  • Copying old masters to understand the multi-dimensional style of painting
