Course I

Bachelor's Academic Course

Specialization
Visual Arts and Restoration
Duration
4 years · full-time program
The programme includes
Training internships, professional practice, pre-diploma internships, final state certification
Certification
Official Diploma awarded upon successful completion

Objective of the course

To form a holistic, analytically meaningful perception of objects and a professional approach to representing the student's figurative solution — mastering the tools of visual display, and studying the typologies of materials and painting techniques. Students learn to detect and attribute objects of material culture, determine their artistic and historical value and category of conservation, organise and carry out research and archive findings, select the optimal model of restoration, and prepare the scientific accounting documentation.

Targets of the course

  • Developing the student's ability of three-dimensional thinking
  • Familiarising with the methodology of realising learning assignments
  • Implementing the image-planning methodology
  • Exploiting oil painting techniques and the expressiveness of tones
  • Studying mixed technique, and the use of drawing within painting
  • Detection, attribution and conservation of objects of material culture
  • Organisation of restoration works and preparation of scientific documentation

Final attestation & enrollment

The quality of practical painting and restoration assignments is assessed by examination. Enrollment for the 1st semester is open until the 15th of August; for the 2nd semester, until the 1st of December.

Career opportunities

Professor of Painting and Drawing, Art Director, professional freelance artist, Paintings Conservator (under a responsible institution), Graphic Designer, and collaborations with the public, magazines, newspapers, museums and galleries.

Visual Arts and Restoration

2 years · full-time program
The programme includes
Professional practice (off-site)
Credits
120 ECTS · 80 credit hours over 2 academic years
Certification
Official Diploma awarded upon successful completion

Objective of the course

The choice of a precise direction for the professional career, and the beginning of working experience in that field.

Targets of the course

  • Application of knowledge by concentrating on practical focus
  • Active participation in professional workshops, master classes and conferences

Final attestation & enrollment

Final attestation of practical assignments is performed by examination. Enrollment for the 1st semester is open until the 15th of August; for the 2nd semester, until the 1st of December.

Course III

PhD Degree

Specialization
Visual Arts and Restoration
Duration
1 year · full-time program
The programme includes
Professional practice
Credits
60 ECTS · 40 credit hours over 1 academic year
Enrollment
Open throughout the academic year

Approach

The course develops step-by-step, from easy to hard, with a constant focus on the analysis of the famous masters' artworks. Theoretical knowledge and methodological guidelines are tested through practical painting and restoration assignments, with midterm attestation and continuous guidance on self-directed work, before a final examination.

Career opportunities

Professor of Painting and Drawing, Art Director, professional freelance artist, Paintings Conservator, Graphic Designer, and collaborations with museums, galleries and the press.

Other programs

Beyond the degree courses

Evening classes, short and summer courses, and master classes — all conducted in English, with a maximum of ten students per class.

Evening School · 1 year

Evening classes

Additional evening classes in Painting, Drawing and other disciplines, held Monday to Friday with three academic hours of practice a day. The didactic program suits different age groups — each student receives an individual approach, with task difficulty matched to their level. Maximum enrollment ten students; an Official Certificate is awarded on completion.

Short courses · from 1 week

Short-term study

For students who wish to evaluate the method and quality of teaching first-hand. You are free to choose the duration — starting from one week — and the time to begin. Full-time study includes six academic hours a day. Enrollment is open throughout the academic year, and an Official Certificate is awarded on completion.

Summer courses · May – October

Summer immersion

An intensive course of deep immersion in the cultural and historical environment of the Renaissance, teaching the fundamentals of drawing, painting, architecture, art history and philosophy. Practical classes are led by professors who graduated from the most prestigious art academies of Russia; art-history lectures and excursions are led by historians with fifteen years of experience in Italy. The week combines studio classes with guided museum visits and a day excursion to Siena.

Workshops & master classes · from 1 week

Workshops & master classes

Throughout the academic year the Academy organises workshops and master classes led by leading instructors from the top artistic universities of Russia, as well as world-famous artists. For the upcoming events program, please write to info@florusart.com.
